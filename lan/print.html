Checklist

Whether your printing an assignment on time or in a hurry, going through a print checklist is essential to make sure mistakes are avoided to save you time and money!

1. Check your bleed

So what is a bleed? A bleed is where your artwork extends to and this is important because when you print and trim yout document, it means that your artwork won’t be cut off when trimmed. Generally, a 3mm bleed is fine but on larger documents this can be increased.

2. Ensure your document is set to CMYK

This includes converting your images ot CMYK also. This will ensure your printed outcome matches the colours and design used in the document.

3. Embed your fonts & links

If your does not have the same fonts as your document then a replacement font will be substituted. To avoid this you can embed your fonts into the PDF. Within Adobe Illustrater you can ‘create outlines’ of your text (after editing) to ensure your text prints as intended.

4. Proof reading

To avoid pixelated and poor quality printed images, It is best to ensure your images are of at least 300 pixels per inch.

5. Image resolutions

Within Adobe programs such as Photoshop, Ilustrator and InDesign, spell checking must be done manually unless you get someone to check your work for. It is essential to avoid unncessary costs.

6. Hard copy proofs

Spending those few extra dollars of hard copy proofs is the best way to assess the future printed outcomes colours, text size and layout.

7. Plan ahead

If your project is complex then it is best to consult with your chosen printing place prior to the deadline. Some places do same day printing but others do not and it also depends on how busy the place is. Best bet is to contact them early to ensure your project can be printed on time.

8. Crop Marks

If you need to trim your document down, save the document with crop marks.

Where to go to get your projects printed? Well, there are many places situated in Melbourne CBD and luckily for us, these can be found using this link printer.lc created by Domenico Mazza, visual designer and PhD student at SensiLab in Melbourne. This resource links you to printers, printer binding services, materials, binding, laser cutting, signage and photo labs in Melbourne’s CBD.

Common places students go to:

The Monsu Workshop
Located on Monash Caulfield campus in building S on level 2. They can do photocopying, scanning, general & large format printing (great for architecture students!), binding and they have a guillotine available too.

Print Corner
From the Caulfield campus it is only a 20-25 minute drive or train and tram ride. Their address is 1/153A High Street, Prahran VIC 3181.

Dinkums
In the centre of Melbourne’s CBD, they have a range of services such as general printing, large format printing, plan printing,stickers & labels and booklet making services to name a few. You can find them at 460 Swanston St, Melbourne VIC 3053.

Whites Law
Provide printing services as well as UV & offset printing, binding services and finishing. You can find them at 802-804 Glenhuntly Rd in Caulfield South VIC 3162.
